Mobile Network Coverage Analysis in Colombia

his project analyzes the evolution of mobile network coverage (2G, 3G, 4G, LTE, and 5G) in Colombia using open government datasets from 2014 to recent years. The analysis includes data cleaning, handling missing values, and exploratory visualizations at the department, municipality, and populated center levels, as well as by main telecom providers.

Project Overview

This project explores the evolution of mobile network coverage (2G, 3G, 4G, LTE, and 5G) in Colombia using open government datasets from 2014 to recent years. The analysis focuses on geographic coverage at the department, municipality, and populated center levels, as well as coverage by major telecom providers.

The main goals are to:

  • Clean and prepare the dataset, addressing missing values such as municipal codes.
  • Explore the adoption of mobile technologies (2G–5G) across time and regions.
  • Visualize coverage distribution among providers and geographic areas.

Dataset

  • Source: Open government data (Ministerio de Tecnologías de la Información y Comunicaciones de Colombia).
  • Years: 2014–present.
  • Variables: Technology type, department, municipality, populated center, provider, and coverage level.

Methodology

  • Data Preparation: Cleaning null values and correcting missing municipality codes (e.g., Mapiripán).
  • Exploratory Analysis: Descriptive statistics and identification of coverage patterns.
  • Visualization: Line plots, bar charts, and geographic comparisons across technologies and providers.

Results

  • Identified gaps in data consistency and corrected missing geographic identifiers.
  • Observed the progressive replacement of 2G/3G networks with 4G/LTE, and the initial expansion of 5G.
  • Visualizations highlight regional differences in connectivity and provider coverage shares.
